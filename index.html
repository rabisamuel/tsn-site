The Sixteenth Night

If you could see the end coming, would you be able to stop yourself from walking toward it?

Buy on Amazon

Synopsis

He was never supposed to meet her. That was the whole point.

The AI system Vijay built reads the shape of a human life, and tells you where it ends. He ran it against the ancient Naadi leaves, the Tamil palm-leaf records that claim every life was written down before it was lived, certain it would find nothing.

It turned and pointed at him.

A woman he had never met. Her death, in his hands.

He is wrong about that. He is wrong about almost everything except her.

In Tamil Nadu, they say your whole life was inscribed on a palm leaf centuries before you were born. Vijay built a machine to prove them wrong.

The Characters

Vijay

Data Scientist

He built an engine to read the shape of a life. It read his first, and then it pointed past him.

Meera

The Woman on the Leaf

Named in a record older than her family's memory. She never asked to be found, or foretold.

Ravi

The Collaborator

He linked the leaves to the machine and never met the woman it named. What he set in motion, he cannot take back.
